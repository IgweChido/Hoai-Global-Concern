Important Tax Features

What is Tax?

A tax is a compulsory charge to be paid by the citizens who are liable to pay it.

What is Levy?

A levy is an obligatory payment to the government or another organization, example; the new Police Fund Levy.

What is Duty?

A duty is a type of tax charged specifically on the value of goods and services, example; Value Added Tax (VAT).

Nigerian Taxes

Taxes to be aware of when doing business in Nigeria

Taxes come in the forms and categories and it is necessary you know exactly what taxes you end up paying in your day to day lives, both knowingly and unknowingly.

Direct and Indirect Taxes

Direct Taxes:

You must have been aware that there are taxes you pay straight to the government based on incomes earned through your business ventures, such as: Company Income Tax (CIT), Education Tax (EDT), Personal Income Tax (PIT), P.A.Y.E etc.

Indirect Taxes:

There are other taxes you pay to the government through intermediaries, for providing goods and/or services, such as Value Added Tax, Withholding Tax, Sales Tax, Excise Duty etc. Please do not confuse some levies in some states as tax. E.g., a toll or an estate levy is not a tax. They are simply fees you pay when you choose to pay for higher level of convenience, reliability or safety. The direct and indirect taxes paid there are three categories in Nigeria: Federal, State and Local taxes.

Federal taxes :

include; Company Income Tax (CIT), Education Tax (EDT), Value Added Tax (VAT), Withholding Tax (WHT), Stamp Duty Tax (SDT) etc.

State taxes :

include; Personal Income Tax (PIT), Pay As You Earn (P.A.Y.E), Consumption Tax (CPT), Business Premises Tax (BPT), Development Levy (Dev Levy) etc.

Local taxes :

These taxes are subject to reviews and changes by the government and new ones can be introduced. Such as the Communication Tax, this has been introduced to the National Assembly for passage into law. It will be imposed on charges payable by consumers of electronic communication services in the country at the rate of 9%.

Nigerian Tax Rates

Corporate Taxes –

Under the exclusive responsibility of the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS)

Company Income Tax (CIT):

0% is to be charged on Taxable Income for small-sized companies with annual turnover of less than or equal to ₦25 million. They are not required to pay the mandatory “minimum tax” on turnover. 20% is to be charged on Taxable Income for medium-sized companies , defined as those between ₦25 million and ₦100 million, will now pay lower company income tax rate of 20%. In the case of minimum tax, they will only pay minimum tax based on 0.5 percent of their turnover. 30% is to be charged on Taxable Income for large companies as usual.

Education Tax (EDT):

2% is to be charged on Assessable Income on medium and large companies.

Withholding Tax (WHT):

5% or 10% retention tax on income from services based on the following considerations:

Types of payment WHT for companies (%) WHT for individuals(%)
Dividends, interest, and rents 10 10
Directors fees N/A 10
Hire of equipment 10 5
Royalties 10 5
Commission, consultancy, technical, service fees 10 5
Management fees 10 5
Construction/building (excluding survey, design, and deliveries) 5 5
Contracts other than sales in the ordinary course of business 5 5

Value Added Tax (VAT):

7.5% is to be charged on goods sold or services provided. Basic food items, agro and aqua based staple foods, including water, are all exempted from VAT. However, the exempted companies will have to file NIL returns for proper identification and record purposes.
Local manufacturing companies producing sanitary pads, towels, tissue papers will not pay VAT anymore but only required to file NIL returns. Educational institutions are exempted from paying VAT on tuition fees (for primary, secondary and tertiary education). Companies with annual turnover of less than or equal to ₦25 million will no longer be required to pay VAT but file NIL returns. Services rendered by Microfinance Banks are exempted from VAT but have to file NIL returns.

Capital Gains Tax (CGT):

10% is charged on capital gains arising from the disposal of capital assets.

Stamp Duties (STD):

There are over 70 stamp duty rates for different transactions and are listed on this link - Nigerian Stamp Duties

Real Property Tax (RPT):

The various taxes that apply on real estate transfer transaction include Capital Gains Tax (CGT), Stamp Duties and Value Added Tax (VAT) (where the property is not undeveloped land).

Individual Taxes –

Under the exclusive responsibility of the States Board of Internal Revenue Service (SIRS) The following taxes- WHT, CGT, STD and RPT are collected from individuals, partnerships and associates by the States Board of Internal Revenue Service (SIRS) at the same rates mentioned under FIRS.

For Personal Income Tax (PIT), the Pay As You Earn (P.A.Y.E) is computed with the under listed rates.

TAX RATE

Personal income tax Progressive rates up to 24%
NGN 0 to NGN 300,000 7%
Next NGN 300,000 11%
Next NGN 500,000 15%
Next NGN 500,000 19%
Next NGN 1,600,000 21%
Above NGN 3,200,000 24%

income, a tax rate of 1% is
applied to the total income
